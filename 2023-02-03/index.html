Background

Background

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{BISP exploring options}}\) for streamlining its \(\color{blue}{\textit{payment}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{model}}\), in light of \(\color{red}{\textit{complaints of deductions by}}\) \(\color{red}{\textit{beneficiaries}}\)

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Payments Mechanisms Committee of BISP Board}}\) directed conducting a \(\color{blue}{\textit{needs assessment study}}\) to obtain \(\color{blue}{\textit{feedback of BISP beneficiaries}}\) on which model would best cater to their requirements

    • \(\color{red}{\textit{Outsource study to a third party firm}}\)

Background

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Policy & Research Unit}}\) assisted CT Wing in \(\color{blue}{\textit{development of design & scope of study}}\) under following two modalities:

    • \(\color{blue}{\textit{Outsourcing study to third party firm}}\)
    • \(\color{green}{\textit{Carry out study in-house using BISP field staff}}\)

Objectives

Objectives

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Assess basic understanding of BISP Kifalat beneficiaries}}\) about \(\color{blue}{\textit{existing}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{payment mechanism}}\)
  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Determine the cost incurred}}\) by an average Kifalat beneficiary in obtaining \(\color{blue}{\textit{quarterly installment}}\), including cost of \(\color{red}{\textit{illegal deductions}}\)
  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Get feedback}}\) of Kifalat beneficiaries with respect to \(\color{green}{\textit{satisfaction}}\) with the existing \(\color{blue}{\textit{payment model}}\)
  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Seek opinion}}\) of Kifalat beneficiaries about \(\color{green}{\textit{feasibility}}\) of introducing \(\color{blue}{\textit{new payment}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{mechanisms}}\)
  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Obtain other information}}\) relevant to the \(\color{blue}{\textit{cost / ease}}\) of obtaining payment by Kifalat beneficiaries

Design & Methodology

Design & Methodology

  • In view of \(\color{red}{\textit{extended timelines involved in engagement of third party}}\), it was decided to \(\color{green}{\textit{conduct study in-house in a rapid assessment manner}}\)

  • Study based on a \(\color{green}{\textit{quantitative sample survey}}\) carried out through BISP tehsil offices across all provinces/ regions

    • \(\color{green}{\textit{Sample universe is the population of existing BISP Kifalat beneficiaries}}\)

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Each Tehsil office}}\) assigned target of collecting data from \(\color{blue}{\textit{15-20 beneficiaries}}\) in their respective areas

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Data collection}}\) carried out through a \(\color{green}{\textit{short questionnaire}}\) developed on \(\color{blue}{\textit{restricted}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{google forms}}\) to enable online collection and reporting of data

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Training on data collection through online form provided}}\) to BISP field staff separately across each province/ region

Limitations

Limitations

  • \(\color{green}{\textit{Survey}}\) carried out using \(\color{blue}{\textit{purposive sampling}}\) due to the \(\color{blue}{\textit{rapid assessment}}\) approach employed in survey implementation
    • Survey results are less \(\color{blue}{\textit{robust}}\) than those obtained on basis of random sampling
  • \(\color{blue}{\textit{Lack of monitoring mechanism}}\) for quality assurance of data collected by field staff
    • \(\color{green}{\textit{Data validation checks}}\) built into survey questionnaire for \(\color{green}{\textit{minimizing}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{data}}\) \(\color{blue}{\textit{entry}}\) and \(\color{blue}{\textit{reporting errors}}\)
  • Survey sample very \(\color{red}{\textit{small}}\) in case of Sindh and Balochistan
    • \(\color{blue}{\textit{Results for these provinces need to be interpreted with more care}}\)
